Significant Patterns Identified

🔁 Sunday–Monday Surge Cycle

Sun avg 5,110 · Mon avg 5,019 tests

AHD consistently spikes on Sunday (Islamic weekend end) and Monday. The 3 Sundays in data (Mar 1, 8, 15) all exceed Tuesday–Wednesday. This is a structural UAE healthcare demand cycle, not noise.

📉 Wednesday Structural Low

Wed avg 4,448 tests — 13% below Mon

Across Mar 1–18, every Wednesday sits below adjacent Tue/Thu. Possible causes: midweek scheduling gaps, physician leave patterns, or patient preference clustering around week-start/end.

⚡ Pre-Holiday Surge Mar 18

5,427 total tests — busiest Wed in dataset

Mar 18 (last working day before Eid holidays) was the highest-ever Wednesday, +22% above the Wed average. Patients front-loaded tests and appointments anticipating clinic closures Mar 19–21.

⚡ Post-Eid Instant Rebound Mar 22

5,507 tests on Day 1 back (Sun)

Mar 22 (Eid Day 2, partial services) produced 5,507 tests — the second highest day in the dataset. Deferred demand released immediately. AHD Main TAT held at 69 min despite surge.

🔴 Satellite TAT — Structural, Not Operational

189 min avg · range 90–280 · no improvement trend

Unlike AHD Main (which improved from 101 to 69 min intra-month), satellite TAT shows zero downward trend over 26 days. This confirms transport lag is the cause — not workload, not staffing. POCT is the only structural fix.

✅ AHD Main TAT Improving

Week 1 avg: 93 min → Week 3: 81 min

AHD Main TAT declined steadily from early-March peak (101 min Mar 4) to consistent 69–85 min in weeks 3–4. Lab workflow optimization or reduced inpatient load. Mar 22 post-Eid: 69 min despite high volume.

📊 Inpatient % Rises Mid-Month

Mar 1–8: 20% IP → Mar 18–22: 25% IP

Outpatient share drops from 66% to 58% in weeks 3–4. Emergency share rose from 8.9% to 12.3%. Suggests heavier acuity load in the second half — possibly seasonal/post-weekend accumulation.

🧬 POCT Demand Concentrated

Rapid Strep: 72% of all satellite POCT

Rapid Strep + Flu A&B account for 85% of satellite POCT demand. This is a single-device deployment: one GeneXpert or similar covers the majority of the POCT clinical need at every clinic. Low complexity, high impact.

📈 Science Park MID Scaling

Jan: ~0% → Mar: 10%+ of MID volume

Science Park's imaging contribution grew from near-zero in January to 56 exams in a single week (Mar 18–22). The growth trajectory suggests SP will exceed 15% of total MID by Q2 2026 without additional capacity.
