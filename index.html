Clinical Insights & Recommendations — Jan–Apr 2026 (4 Months Complete): Analysis covers Medical Imaging (MID) and Laboratory performance for January–April 2026 vs same period 2025. April was the standout month — MID recovered strongly to 11,475 studies (+27.8% vs March, +0.9% vs Apr 2025), and Lab delivered its highest month of 2026 at 208,677 tests (+31.5% vs March, +12.6% vs Apr 2025). Post-Ramadan demand surge confirmed across all major sections and modalities.

🟢 MID — Growth Drivers (Jan–Apr)

  • 🥇MRI — Strongest April of 2026 (1,509): Jan 1,419 → Feb 1,334 → Mar 1,132 → Apr 1,509. April +33.2% vs March, +26.5% vs Apr 2025 (1,193). Sustained growth confirms rising referral demand.
  • 🏅IR-CathLab April: 39 studies (+200% vs Apr 2025 at 13): Q1 nearly doubled YoY. April consolidates the new elevated baseline.
  • 📊CT April Recovery (+24.1% vs March): Bounced to 1,345 from March low (1,084). YoY +11.7% vs Apr 2025 (1,204).
  • 🏥Science Park MID Surge (+68.2% MoM in April): 1,009 in March → 1,696 in April. SP now 14.8% of total MID, up from 11.2%. Strong satellite growth signal.
  • 🔭Nuclear Medicine April: 108 vs 92 in March (+17.4%). PET-CT holding at 111 vs 112 in Apr 2025 (−0.9%) — stable oncology pipeline.

🔴 MID — Areas Needing Action (Jan–Apr)

  • ⚠️Ultrasound YoY Gap Persists: April 2,884 vs Apr 2025 3,752 (−23.1% YoY). Despite April MoM recovery (+18%), the YoY deficit is the largest absolute gap in MID. Satellite US down −28.1%. Referral pipeline review needed urgently.
  • ⚠️X-Ray Still Below 2025: April 3,490 vs Apr 2025 4,081 (−14.5% YoY). Q1 was −5.6%. The YoY gap is widening — X-Ray is the single biggest absolute volume risk across the 4-month period.
  • ⚠️DEXA Structural Decline: Jan 233 → Feb 176 → Mar 150 → Apr 238. April partially recovered but still −19.3% below Apr 2025 (295). Endocrinology/Ortho referral pipeline requires sustained attention.
  • 📉Fluoroscopy 4-Month Trend: Jan 158 → Feb 122 → Mar 143 → Apr 147. Slight stabilisation in April but still −20.5% below Apr 2025 (185). Scope change context should be reviewed for accurate benchmarking.

🟢 Laboratory — Growth & Strengths (Jan–Apr)

  • 🥇April: Strongest Lab Month of 2026 — 208,677 tests: +31.5% vs March (158,681) and +12.6% vs Apr 2025 (185,303). Chemistry (+31.9%), Endocrinology (+49.4%), Immunology (+52.7%), Hematology (+24.3%) all surged — broad-based post-Ramadan recovery.
  • 🧪Chemistry April Record — 160,925 tests: +16.1% YoY vs Apr 2025 (138,603). 4-month Chemistry total: 554,777 tests — holds 75.9% share of all lab tests across the period.
  • 📊4-Month Lab Total: 730,172 tests (+6.8% vs Jan–Apr 2025). Every month in 2026 exceeded the 2025 comparative. Consistent growth trajectory confirms structural demand increase.
  • 📈Endocrinology April: 10,738 tests (+49.4% vs March, +26.6% vs Apr 2025). Strongest April performance across all non-Chemistry sections. Reflects strong post-Ramadan hormone testing demand.
  • 🏥Outreach Referrals: Jan 271 → Feb 316 → Mar 502 → Apr ~600. Consistent upward trajectory. Must be sustained and accelerated to close the gap with 2025 benchmarks.

🔴 Laboratory — Concerns & Watch Items (Jan–Apr)

  • ⚠️Molecular Persistent Decline: Jan 2,444 → Feb 2,294 → Mar 1,295 → Apr 1,474. Despite April partial recovery, April is −39.6% below Apr 2025 (2,438). 4-month Molecular total down ~24% YoY. This is now structural — not a one-month anomaly. MSR_2110_02146 (Molecular Technologist, Apr 2026 budget) must be filled immediately.
  • ⚠️POC Structural Decline: Jan 11,098 → Feb 9,807 → Mar 6,348 → Apr 8,308. April recovered vs March but still −25.0% below Apr 2025 (11,076). 4-month POC total down ~22% YoY. ER/urgent care utilization patterns should be reviewed.
  • 📉Blood Bank April: 1,212 vs Apr 2025: 1,324 (−8.5% YoY). Consistently below 2025 across all 4 months. Inpatient surgical volumes or blood product ordering patterns may have changed.
  • 📉Satellite Clinic Divergence: MSC and DHC declining both MoM and YoY across Q1 and into April. NSC and ABC outperforming. Root cause analysis needed for underperforming clinics — may reflect staffing, competition, or patient flow changes.

🎯 Top 6 Recommended Actions — Based on Jan–Apr 2026 Complete Data

  • 1️⃣Protect & Grow MRI Momentum: MRI April was the best month of 2026 at 1,509 studies (+26.5% YoY). Assess additional slot capacity, extended hours, and protocol optimisation. MRI should be the strategic imaging growth driver through Q2–Q3 2026.
  • 2️⃣Urgent: Address Ultrasound YoY Gap (−23.1% April YoY): The largest absolute volume deficit across MID. Satellite US down −28.1%. Conduct a referral source audit — identify the top 20 referring physicians who have reduced US orders. Deploy targeted outreach within 30 days.
  • 3️⃣X-Ray Referral Recovery Initiative: YoY gap widened to −14.5% in April. With X-Ray representing ~30% of total imaging volume, a targeted referral recovery and scheduling accessibility review is critical.
  • 4️⃣Sustain April Lab Momentum into May: April's 208,677 tests is a post-Ramadan peak that may normalise in May. Monitor Chemistry, Endocrinology, and Immunology weekly to ensure volumes hold above March baselines and the April trajectory is sustained.
  • 5️⃣Escalate Molecular Resolution: 4-month total down ~24% vs prior year. April 1,474 vs Apr 2025 2,438 (−39.6%). This is now a structural deficit. MSR_2110_02146 (Molecular Technologist, April 2026 budget) is overdue and must be filled immediately.
  • 6️⃣Science Park Scale-Up: SP MID surged +68.2% MoM in April (1,696 studies). SP Lab also growing strongly. The satellite is meeting suppressed demand — evaluate staffing, equipment, and scheduling capacity increases at SP to capitalise on this accelerating growth.

📈 Multi-Year Growth Context — AHD Diagnostic Center

  • 📌2023 Imaging: 85,766 studies — Baseline year.
  • 📌2024 Imaging: 106,738 studies (+24.4%) — Exceptional expansion. MRI +28.7%, CT +19.8%, US +25.0%.
  • 📌2025 Imaging: 115,833 studies (+8.5%) — Sustainable growth. IR accelerated (+30.7%), PET-CT +17.1%.
  • 📌Jan–Apr 2026 Imaging: 41,746 studies (+2.7% vs Jan–Apr 2025): Q1 was flat (−0.01%) but April recovered to +0.9% YoY (11,475 vs 11,378). April confirms the volume floor has been reached and upward recovery is underway.
  • 📌Jan–Apr 2026 Lab: 730,172 tests (+6.8% vs Jan–Apr 2025): Lab growth is consistent and broad-based. April's 208,677 tests is the strongest month of 2026 and the clearest indicator of post-Ramadan demand normalisation.
  • 🔑Key Takeaway: After Q1 imaging stagnation (driven by March Ramadan suppression), April's recovery across both divisions confirms that demand fundamentals are strong. Priority: sustain MRI and Chemistry growth, close the Ultrasound and Molecular gaps, and capitalise on Science Park's accelerating performance heading into Q2 2026.
